About me.

I pair critical theory with machine learning to design equitable knowledge tools for communities and cultural stewards.

I am María-Teresa Carmier—a machine learning researcher, designer, and storyteller. My practice lives at the intersection of responsible AI and cultural heritage: I prototype systems that make archives searchable, legible, and just for the communities they document.

Currently Data Science Lab, UC Berkeley

Architecting retrieval and evaluation pipelines that power humanities-facing AI pilots across the university.

Research Interests Algorithmic accountability & multimodal storytelling

How do we audit narrative AI experiences, and how can we encode community governance inside creative tooling?

Collaborators Museums, libraries & civic technologists

Recent partners include the Bancroft Library, Oakland Public Library, and the Algorithmic Fairness & Opacity Working Group.

Biography

Trained in computational linguistics and media theory, I translate scholarship into deployable software. Prior to the Data Science Lab, I led research sprints at an Oakland civic-tech nonprofit, where we produced open-source datasets for local journalists and community organizers. Earlier, I studied comparative literature and computer science at UC Berkeley, which sharpened my obsession with how language, archives, and power collide in machine learning.

Across every project, I obsess over the full lifecycle: mapping messy ingestion pipelines, aligning models with stakeholder values, and crafting interfaces that invite curiosity rather than extract attention. I am equally comfortable pair programming ETL jobs as I am facilitating participatory design workshops with librarians and curators.

Principles I work by

  • Context over convenience. Data inherits politics; my tooling foregrounds provenance, consent, and community ownership.
  • Prototypes as arguments. I prototype quickly to test ethics, usability, and feasibility in parallel—allowing stakeholders to interrogate tradeoffs early.
  • Measure what matters. Beyond latency and accuracy, my scorecards track narrative diversity, representation, and long-term stewardship capacity.

Selected collaborations

  • Designed a retrieval-augmented storytelling service for the Bancroft Library that indexes 18 TB of oral histories and surfaces related artifacts in under 1.2 seconds.
  • Co-created an AI accountability playbook for regional museums; the rubric now informs procurement decisions across five partner institutions.
  • Led speculative prototyping workshops with Oakland youth to imagine sensor-driven climate data murals, feeding requirements into the Black Eco Feminisms atlas.
I’m actively exploring collaborations that center cultural memory, civic technology, and humane AI. Let’s build tooling that is rigorous, joyful, and accountable.
