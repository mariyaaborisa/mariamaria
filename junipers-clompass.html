1.

Juniper's Clompass

A responsible story-generation companion for young readers

Juniper’s Clompass is a multimodal narrative engine that helps 8–12 year-olds explore folklore responsibly. I led the ML system design, building a pipeline that pairs curated cultural datasets with safety guardrails and playful voice interfaces.

Role Lead ML Researcher & Product Strategist

Directed data strategy, model tuning, and facilitated co-design workshops with educators.

Timeline Jan – Jun 2024

Three sprint cycles across discovery, prototyping, and pilot deployment.

Stack PyTorch · Hugging Face · FastAPI · Redis · ElevenLabs

Fine-tuned instruction models, streaming inference via FastAPI, and real-time caching for parent dashboards.

Scale 1K weekly story sessions

Deployed across two after-school programs with multilingual support (EN/ES).

Design challenge

Children’s literature discovery tools often flatten cultural nuance and replicate harmful stereotypes. Our partner schools wanted an AI companion that could surface underrepresented folklore while offering caregivers transparent controls. We needed a system that balanced imagination with rigorous guardrails.

What we built

  • Curated a bilingual corpus of 12k folklore narratives with metadata capturing region, protagonist identity, and teachable themes.
  • Fine-tuned Mistral-7B using LoRA adapters to output structured story beats (setup, conflict, resolution) constrained by educator-approved tropes.
  • Layered a narrative safety harness that evaluates generated content for bias, reading level, and emotional intensity before release.
  • Designed a caregiver dashboard showing the provenance of each story and explaining why specific guardrails were triggered.
Fine-tuned LLMs Multi-layer safety Multilingual (EN/ES) Caregiver transparency

Safety instrumentation

Safety is a multi-model conversation. We chained OpenAI moderation, a BERT-based stereotype classifier we trained on 48k annotations, and a custom lexicon for cultural slurs. Stories failing any check are automatically revised with transparent rationale shared to caregivers.

Impact

  • Story diversity scores increased by 68% compared to the district’s legacy e-book platform.
  • Educators reported a 42% uptick in student-led discussions about cultural values during storytelling hour.
  • Zero high-severity safety incidents occurred across the first 9 weeks of deployment.

What I’d explore next

  • Introduce retrieval-based citations so every generated beat links back to the source tales and annotations.
  • Release an open benchmarking suite for cultural narrative quality to help other youth storytelling tools measure impact.
  • Prototype tablet-native creation tools for students to author their own folklore and feed the corpus.
