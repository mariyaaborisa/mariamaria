Show Me your colors

Graduate Thesis Project

Show Me Your Colors is an AI-driven wearable system that helps children understand and express their emotions through color. Combining physical computing with machine learning, the project translates physiological signals into visual emotional feedback, creating a bridge between internal feelings and external expression.

Domain AI · Wearable Tech · Child Development

Merging emotion recognition with tangible interaction design for young learners.

Experience Emotion-sensing wearable

Children wear sensors that detect emotional states and display colors representing their feelings.

Stack Arduino · Biometric Sensors · Machine Learning

Real-time physiological data processing with visual feedback systems.

Focus Graduate Thesis Project

Exploring how AI can support emotional intelligence development in children.

Project overview

Emotional state prototypes

Early design explorations used familiar metaphors like ice cream to represent emotional states—full (complete), melting (transitioning), and melted (depleted)—helping children visualize emotional changes.

Co-creation with children

Through participatory design sessions, children helped shape how emotions should be represented. They created emotion cards, explored color associations, and tested wearable prototypes to ensure the system resonated with their lived experiences.

Emotion indicators in action

Impact

This project demonstrates how AI can amplify—rather than replace—human emotional understanding, creating tools that help children build vocabulary around feelings and develop emotional literacy.
