Black Eco Feminisms

Land Back with Conditions: Ninth Root Internship

As part of my Berkeley summer fieldwork requirement, I worked with with Ninth Root, an Oakland-based environmental justice nonprofit. The internship asked how civic design tools could protect Black land stewardship when development pressures and ecological harm collide.

Environmental Justice Urban Planning Civic Design

Project overview

In the summer of 24, I completed an Urban Planning Internship with Ninth Root, an Oakland-based environmental justice nonprofit. I worked alongside community organizers and residents to design listening sessions that addressed land use, displacement, and ecological care. This project investigated how civic design tools—such as systems mapping, co-creation workshops, and storytelling—could strengthen community agency in environmental planning and stewardship.

Key contributions

  • Community Listening Sessions: Designed and facilitated sessions centering intergenerational narratives around environmental harm, healing, and land justice.
  • Civic Design Strategy: Mapped relationships between community assets, governance structures, and environmental inequities to inform participatory planning.
  • Workshop Design: Co-created creative engagements that merged art, policy dialogue, and local activism to translate residents’ stories into actionable frameworks.
  • Training & Visual Tools: Produced volunteer recruitment materials, facilitation guides, and visual systems maps to support Ninth Root’s ongoing initiatives.

Ongoing scholarship

This fieldwork informs my forthcoming chapter on Black Ecofeminisms and Land Stewardship in Indigenous Sovereignty in Environmental Research: Decolonizing Ethics and Methodologies (Routledge, forthcoming). The chapter extends this applied design work, positioning stewardship as both a civic design practice and a framework of care within environmental and technological systems.

Impact

Turned community listening into civic intelligence—translating lived experiences into design artifacts that guide equitable environmental planning and future coalition-building.
